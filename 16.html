#16 - Shantungosaurus

Name: Shantungosaurus giganteus, "giant lizard from Shantung”

Family: Hadrosauridae (duck billed, bird hipped dinosaurs, typically herbivorous)

Lived: Late Cretaceous (75 to 70 million years ago), China

First Described: 1973, China

Size: Approx. 52ft long, 16ft tall, and weighed 35,000lbs to 40,000lbs


Shantungosaurus is the largest non-sauropod dinosaur by weight. This is the main reason for its inclusion on this list, but there are some other unique traits of Shantungosaurus aside from that. For one, it’s well understood that Shantungosaurus ate meat, not as a carnivore, but whenever the opportunity arose. That means that, despite being a herbivore, it wouldn’t pass up on hunting and killing smaller animals if it was hungry enough - and when you’re a Shantungosaurus, everything is a smaller animal.
