#18 - Nothosaurus

Name: Nothosaurus mirabilis, "amazing false lizard‚Äù

Family: Sauropterygia (aquatic reptiles, not dinosaurs, which had flippers and evolved from terrestrial reptiles)

Lived: Middle Triassic (240 to 210 million years ago), across Europe, Asia, and Africa

First Described: 1834, Germany

Size: Approx. 13ft long, 3ft tall, and weighed 600lbs


Nothosaurus was a lithe aqualic reptile that is typically depicted as either sunbathing on rocks, a theoretical behavior similar to seals, or diving into shallow waters to eat fish. They were possibly an early ancestor of plesiosaurs, evolving to live in the water full time, as evidenced by a body structure which resembles that of plesiosaurs, just capable of walking on land. Nothosaurus came much before even the most primitive plesiosaur species, though.
